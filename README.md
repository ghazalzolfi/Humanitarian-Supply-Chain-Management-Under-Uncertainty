# Humanitarian-Supply-Chain-Management-Under-Uncertainty

## 📌 Project Overview
This repository contains the study materials, presentation files, and a comprehensive report on **Humanitarian Supply Chain Management (HSCM) Under Uncertainty**. This project was developed for the "Fundamentals of Supply Chain Management" course at Sharif University of Technology (Winter 2026).

Unlike commercial supply chains that primarily focus on cost efficiency and profit maximization, humanitarian logistics prioritize human welfare, equity, and the rapid delivery of life-saving relief. The core objective of this study is to explore how supply chains can effectively operate under the severe uncertainties caused by natural disasters and crises.

## 🔑 Key Concepts Explored
Our research and presentation cover the following critical areas:

* **HSCM vs. Commercial Logistics:** An analysis of how humanitarian models differ from commercial ones, particularly in dealing with highly unpredictable and urgent demand patterns.
* **Challenges of Uncertainty:** An overview of the main sources of uncertainty in disaster relief, including demand unpredictability, supply volatility (donor dependency), and infrastructure disruptions.
* **Deprivation Cost Function:** A detailed look into the economic quantification of human suffering caused by the delayed delivery of critical goods. This cost increases non-linearly and convexly over time.
* **The Crisis Life Cycle:** Strategies for different phases of a disaster:
  * *Pre-crisis:* Mitigation, preparedness, and strategic facility location.
  * *During crisis:* Real-time response, dynamic routing, and equity-based resource allocation.
  * *Post-crisis:* Recovery, reverse logistics for debris removal, and sustainable reconstruction.
* **Mathematical Modeling Approaches:** A review of methods used to address uncertainty, such as stochastic programming, robust optimization, and distributionally robust optimization.
* **Case Study (Gulf Hurricane):** An examination of Zhang & Cui's (2021) framework for pre-positioning emergency equipment, demonstrating how integrating deprivation costs impacts strategic facility placement and resource allocation.
